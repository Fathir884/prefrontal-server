## 3. Module 2: Home Dashboard (The Command Center)

### ðŸ§  Conceptual Overview
The Dashboard is designed for "Cognitive Ergonomics". Its goal is to provide maximum situational awareness with minimum visual clutter. It answers the question: *"How am I doing right now?"*

### âœ¨ Key Features
1.  **Dynamic Greeting:** The app recognizes the time of day and greets you accordingly, often with a stoic quote to ground your mindset.
2.  **Vital Signs Cards:** These are three live data streams:
    *   *Financial Pulse:* Your Net Balance (Income - Expense). Green means surplus, Red means deficit.
    *   *Task Load:* The number of incomplete tasks due today.
    *   *Mental Streak:* How many days in a row you have logged your mood.
3.  **Quick Actions:** A grid of color-coded buttons to jump to specific deep modules without navigation fatigue.

### ðŸ›  Step-by-Step Usage Guide
1.  **Open the App:** You land here by default.
2.  **Check Vital Signs:** Glance at the top cards. If "Financial Pulse" is Red, you know to be frugal today. If "Task Load" is high (>5), you know to focus on work.
3.  **Navigation:** Tap any colorful square in the "Quick Actions" grid to jump to that module (e.g., Tap `Wallet` icon to go to Finance).

## 4. Module 3: Calendar & Tasks (Temporal Architecture)

### ðŸ§  Conceptual Overview
Time management often fails because we treat "Meetings" (Fixed Time) and "To-Dos" (Flexible Time) as the same thing. Prefrontal separates them into a **Dual-System Architecture**.

### âœ¨ Key Features
1.  **The Event Calendar:** This is for your "Hard Landscape"â€”things that *must* happen at a specific time (e.g., Dentist appointment, Flight).
2.  **The Task List:** This is for your "Soft Landscape"â€”things that need to get done but have flexible execution windows.
3.  **Smart Notifications:** Local system notifications trigger locally on your device, ensuring privacy and reliability even offline.

### ðŸ›  Step-by-Step Usage Guide

#### A. Adding an Event (Fixed Time)
1.  Navigate to the **Calendar** tab.
2.  Tap the **"+" (Plus)** button.
3.  Select **"Event"** tab.
4.  Enter Title (e.g., "Team Meeting").
5.  Select Date and Time using the picker.
6.  Toggle **"Remind Me"** if you want a push notification.
7.  Tap **Save**. A dot will appear on the calendar date.

#### B. Adding a Task (Flexible)
1.  In the **Calendar** tab, scroll down to the "Tasks" list.
2.  Tap **"Add Task"**.
3.  Enter Description (e.g., "Finish Math Homework").
4.  Select Priority:
    *   *High (Red):* Do it today.
    *   *Medium (Yellow):* Do it soon.
    *   *Low (Blue):* Do it whenever.
5.  Tap **Save**.
6.  **To Complete:** Tap the empty circle/checkbox. Feel the haptic feedback as it crosses out.

## 5. Module 4: Learning Center (Focus Engine)

### ðŸ§  Conceptual Overview
This module facilitates "Deep Work" and "Active Recall". It combines a Timer (to manage energy) with Soundscapes (to manage environment) and Flashcards (to manage memory).

### âœ¨ Key Features
1.  **Pomodoro 2.0:** A customizable timer that tracks "Focus Minutes".
2.  **Focus Soundscapes:** High-fidelity audio loops (Rain, Forest, Fire, White Noise) that mask distracting background frequencies.
3.  **Active Recall Flashcards:** A digital version of physical flashcards with a flip animation.

### ðŸ›  Step-by-Step Usage Guide

#### A. Starting a Focus Session
1.  Go to **Learning** module.
2.  **Select Sound:** Tap the headphone icon and choose "Rain" or "Forest". Adjust volume.
3.  **Select Duration:** Tap "25" (Standard) or "45" (Deep) minutes.
4.  Tap **Start**. The timer begins counting down.
5.  *Tip:* Put your phone face down. The audio will continue playing in the background.

#### B. Creating & Using Flashcards
1.  Switch to the **"Flashcards"** tab within Learning.
2.  Tap **"New Card"**.
3.  **Front:** Type the question (e.g., "What is the capital of France?").
4.  **Back:** Type the answer (e.g., "Paris").
5.  Tap **Save**.
6.  **To Study:** Tap the card to flip it and reveal the answer. Swipe to move to the next card.

## 6. Module 5: Financial Cortex (Behavioral Economics)

### ðŸ§  Conceptual Overview
Money is emotional. Specifically, humans suffer from "Loss Aversion". This module visually tracks the flow of money to hack your psychology into better spending habits.

### âœ¨ Key Features
1.  **IDR Optimization:** Built for Indonesia. Inputs auto-format to `Rp 1.000.000`. No decimals.
2.  **Speed Logging:** Designed to be used while standing in line at the cashier (3 seconds max).
3.  **Analytics:** Donut charts show category breakdown; Trend lines show spending velocity.

### ðŸ›  Step-by-Step Usage Guide

#### A. Logging a Transaction
1.  Go to **Finance** module.
2.  Tap **"Add Transaction"**.
3.  **Amount:** Type the number (e.g., 50000).
4.  **Type:** Select "Expense" (Red) or "Income" (Green).
5.  **Category:** Choose icon (Food, Transport, Bill, etc.).
6.  Tap **Save**. Your "Net Balance" updates instantly.

#### B. Viewing Analytics
1.  Tap the **"Analytics"** tab at the top.
2.  View the **Donut Chart** to see where most of your money went (e.g., 40% Food).
3.  Check the **Recent History** list to find specific past transactions.
